Why Leadership Matters

What the choice between Amy Acton and Vivek Ramaswamy means for the people of Ohio

Published: March 2026

Why does the choice of governor matter so deeply?

Because the person leading Ohio does more than run an administration. They shape how the state responds to crisis, how it invests in its communities, and whether decisions are driven by evidence, ideology, or something in between.

And that ultimately determines what everyday life looks like for the people of Ohio.

What Should Ohioans Expect From Their Governor?

When voters evaluate leadership, several basic questions emerge.

  • Should decisions affecting millions be grounded in expertise?
  • Should government institutions be strengthened or dismantled?
  • When a crisis occurs, should leaders rely on scientific guidance or personal philosophy?

These questions frame the difference between two very different figures: Dr. Amy Acton and Vivek Ramaswamy.

What Kind of Experience Prepares Someone to Govern?

Amy Acton is a physician and public health expert who served as Director of the Ohio Department of Health.

During the early months of the COVID-19 pandemic, she helped guide Ohio's response alongside Republican Governor Mike DeWine. Her role involved analyzing emerging scientific evidence, coordinating with hospitals and local health departments, and communicating directly with the public.

Her background is rooted in:

  • Medicine
  • Epidemiology
  • Public health leadership

In other words, the systems designed to protect the health of communities.

Vivek Ramaswamy's path is very different.

He is an entrepreneur who founded the pharmaceutical company Roivant Sciences, built a national political profile during the 2024 presidential primary, and has become known for his critiques of government institutions and regulatory structures.

His background centers on:

  • Business entrepreneurship
  • Political commentary
  • Institutional reform

So the question becomes: which type of experience better prepares someone to lead a state government?

How Should Government Respond to Crisis?

Ohio, like every state, faces unpredictable challenges.

  • Public health emergencies
  • Economic shocks
  • Environmental risks
  • Infrastructure failures

When those moments arrive, leadership style matters.

Dr. Acton's approach emphasizes listening to scientific expertise, building consensus across institutions, and making decisions based on available data.

Ramaswamy has argued that many institutional responses — particularly during the pandemic — represented government overreach and that administrative agencies should wield far less authority.

The question for voters: When a crisis hits, should leadership prioritize institutional expertise or individual skepticism toward those institutions?

What Kind of Government Does Ohio Need?

Another difference lies in how each figure views government itself.

Acton's career has worked largely within public systems. She has emphasized strengthening public health infrastructure, investing in preventative care, and coordinating state and local responses.

Ramaswamy has built his political identity around challenging what he describes as bureaucratic overreach. His proposals often focus on shrinking regulatory structures and transferring more power away from government institutions.

Which vision better serves Ohio?

  • One that reinforces public systems to address long-term challenges?
  • Or one that reduces the role those systems play?

What Are the Stakes for Ohio's Future?

Ohio is navigating real and difficult challenges:

  • Rural hospital closures
  • Public health disparities
  • Economic competition with neighboring states
  • Workforce development and education

Each of these issues requires sustained leadership.

The question is not simply who is more charismatic or who dominates headlines. The question is: what philosophy of leadership best positions Ohio for the future?

The Question Every Voter Must Answer

Leadership is ultimately about responsibility.

  • Responsibility to protect communities.
  • Responsibility to govern fairly.
  • Responsibility to make decisions grounded in reality rather than ideology.

When Ohioans step into the voting booth, they are not only choosing a candidate. They are choosing the governing philosophy that will shape the state's future.

And that is why the decision matters.

Because the choice between Amy Acton and Vivek Ramaswamy is ultimately a choice about how Ohio should be led — and what that leadership means for the people who call the state home.
